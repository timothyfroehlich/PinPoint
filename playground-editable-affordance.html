How real consumer sites do it

For the average person on mixed devices, the subtle hover-reveal you have today (A) is the worst fit — invisible on touch. Consumer apps lean to C (obvious fields) or D (explicit control). Your glowing-field idea is a branded version of C.

A
Subtle hover-reveal · Notion, Linear, Coda, Asana
Plain text; hover shows a faint block/controls; click to edit. Clean, but invisible on touch & low-discoverability. = presets "Notion", "Today".
B
Spreadsheet grid · Airtable, Google Sheets, Excel Online, Notion DBs
Visible cell borders; the grid itself says "all editable." Great for dense data; reads as a spreadsheet. = preset "Airtable / Sheets".
C
Always a form field · Google Forms editor, Typeform builder, settings pages
Always-visible inputs; no view/edit split. Zero ambiguity, best for novices; visually heavy ("boxes everywhere"). = preset "Google Forms".
D
Explicit edit control · YouTube Studio, Trello, Gmail signature, X "Edit profile", Spotify
An obvious affordance/button; separates view from edit. Discoverable; costs a click / pencils everywhere. = preset "Explicit pencil".
E
Tap-to-caret · Google Docs, Apple Notes, Word Online
No affordance — click and a caret appears. Works only because the whole surface is a document.
